China

Nestled in the core of East Asia, China's rich shade of culture, cuisine, and captivating sights beckons trippers from every corner of the globe. All about Forbidden City in Beijing

Step into the ancient China as you enter the Forbidden City, a majestic complex nestled in the heart of Beijing. With its towering walls and intricate palaces, this UNESCO World Heritage site stands as a testament of China's royal past. For centuries, it served as the exclusive residence of Chinese emperors and their courts, hidden in mystery and off-limits to the outside world. Today, visitors from across the globe come to see its ornate architecture, courtyards, and priceless treasures housed within its hallowed halls. As you pass through its grand corridors and expansive gardens, you are transported back in time to an era of dynastic splendor and intrigue. The Forbidden City not only preserves China's rich history but also serves as a reminder of the importance of safeguarding cultural heritage for future generations.




One of the famous Chinese Food

Chinese Hot Pot

Explore the culinary adventure through the heart of China with the iconic and yummy hot pot dish, a beloved tradition that brings friends and family together around bubbling pots of broth. Hot pot started long ago in China, hot pot has evolved into a cherished communal dining experience celebrated across the globe. At the center of the table, a simmering cauldron of savory broth awaits, infused with spices, herbs, and aromatics that tantalize the senses. You can select from a tantalizing array of thinly sliced meats, fresh seafood, fresh vegetables, and handmade dumplings, each morsel ready to be cooked to perfection in the simmering broth. As chopsticks dance and conversation flows, the hot pot experience foster bonds and create cherished memories. Hot pot isn't just about food; it's also about being together and making happy memories, all while upholding the principles of dignity and respect for all. It's like a yummy adventure where everyone can try different foods.

Exploring the Chinese New Year Festival

Traditions

Welcome to the vibrant and exciting celebration of Chinese New Year, a happy occasion steeped in centuries-old traditions and cultural significance. As the most important festival in Chinese culture, it marks the beginning of a new lunar year with festivities that span over two weeks. Everywhere you look like streets adorned with colorful lanterns, bustling markets filled with auspicious decorations, and the yummy aroma of traditional food fill the air, creating an atmosphere of excitement and renewal. Families gather to honor their ancestors, exchange red envelopes filled with (Money)blessings, and indulge in big feasts symbolizing prosperity and luckk. Dragon and lion dances captivate spectators, symbolizing good fortune and warding off evil spirits. Through the jubilant festivities, Chinese New Year not only celebrates cultural heritage but also emphasizes the values of family unity, respect, and reflecting the principles of human rights for all.
